    What to Expect:

    The PADI Open Water Diver course is a globally recognized certification program designed to introduce beginners to the world of scuba diving.
    Here's what you can expect from the course:


    Academic Sessions:You'll complete six academic sessions, which can be done online or in a classroom setting. These sessions cover essential scuba diving knowledge, including dive theory, equipment, and safety procedures.

    Confined Water Session:You'll practice your new skills in a controlled environment, such as a pool, with six sessions to ensure you're comfortable with the basics.

    Open Water Dives:You'll complete four open water dives in a local dive site. These dives allow you to apply what you've learned in a real-world setting and gain practical experience. This certification allows you to dive independently with a buddy to the same depth as the training dives.

    Maximum Training Depth:The course includes dives up to a maximum depth of 18 meters (60 feet).

    Maximum Training Depth:The entire course typically takes between 16 to 32 hours to complete.


    Certification:
    Upon successful completion of the course, you'll earn the PADI Open Water Diver certification, which is recognized worldwide.
